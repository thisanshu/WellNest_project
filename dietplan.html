"Let food be thy medicine and medicine be thy food."

~Hippocrates

A nutritious and well-balanced diet is essential for maintaining both our physical and mental health.
On the other hand, diets heavy in processed foods, sweets, and bad fats have been connected to a higher risk of mental health conditions like anxiety, depression, and cognitive loss. People may fuel their brains and foster mental resilience by eating a diet high in nutrients and balanced in macronutrients. Additionally, a number of nutrients have been linked to a decrease in mental illness symptoms and an improvement in mood control, including magnesium, omega-3 fatty acids, and vitamins B and D. Even though diet alone may not be able to treat mental illness, it may be an important part of holistic treatment approaches that work in conjunction with other therapies like counseling and medicine. A balanced diet promotes mental clarity, emotional stability, and an overall higher quality of life in addition to physical health.


Processed foods and sugar can cause inflammation in the brain and throughout the body, which can aggravate mood disorders including sadness and anxiety. Processed meals are frequently what we turn to when we're anxious or sad and need a fast pick-me-up.The American Dietetic Association claims that when people are stressed or sad, they typically overeat or undereat.
The American Psychological Association claims that a variety of neurochemicals produced by gut bacteria are utilized by the brain to control many mental and physiological functions, including mood.

Brain Food

The top three foods to incorporate into a healthy mental diet are :

Complex Carbohydrates: Foods high in starchy vegetables and brown grains offer a long-term supply of energy. Conversely, alternatives like quinoa, millet, beets, and sweet potatoes are higher in nutrients and provide sustained fullness than simple carbs found in candies and sugary foods.

Lean Proteins: In addition, they supply the energy required for quick decisions and activities. High-quality protein sources include fish, poultry, eggs, soybeans, almonds, and seeds.

Fatty Acids: For the nervous system and brain to function at their best, these nutrients are necessary. Seafood, meat, eggs, nuts, and flaxseeds are all abundant in them.
